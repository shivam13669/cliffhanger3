Kerala Tour Gods Own Country

About Kerala tour :

Have you ever heard of Kerala? It’s a stunning place in Southern India that people call “God’s Own Country.” Why? Because it’s filled with beautiful landscapes, rich culture, and amazing wildlife. This place is like a dream come true for anyone looking for a relaxing and exciting vacation.

Discovering the Beauty

Kerala is famous for its backwaters, beaches, and hill stations. Imagine floating on a traditional houseboat, surrounded by the peaceful waters of Alleppey and Kumarakom. It’s a one-of-a-kind experience that lets you soak in the beauty of nature at your own pace.

Wellness and Relaxation

But that’s not all! Kerala is also known for its Ayurvedic treatments and therapies. You can pamper yourself with holistic wellness practices that will leave you feeling rejuvenated and refreshed. It’s like a spa day, but even better!

Immersing in Culture

And let’s not forget about the vibrant festivals and delicious food in Kerala. The people here are so welcoming and friendly, making you feel right at home. You’ll get to experience a whole new world of traditions and flavors that will leave you wanting more.

Conclusion

Kerala truly lives up to its name as God’s Own Country. It’s a paradise just waiting to be explored, with so much beauty and excitement to offer. So why not pack your bags and embark on a magical journey to Kerala? You won’t regret it!

Itinerary

  • DAY 1 - ARRIVE IN COCHIN

    Upon arrival at Cochin International Airport, you will be transferred to your hotel. In the evening, you will have the opportunity to visit the iconic Chinese fishing nets, St. Francis Church, and the Jewish Synagogue. After the tour, you will return to your hotel for an overnight stay in Cochin.

  • DAY 2 - COCHIN TO MUNNAR

    After breakfast, drive to Munnar, a charming hill station known for its picturesque tea gardens. Witness local women delicately plucking tea leaves while engaging in lively conversations. Along the way, visit breathtaking waterfalls, the Maddupatty dam, and a tea factory. Spend the night in Munnar, surrounded by the beauty of nature.

  • DAY 3 - HIKE NATIONAL PARK MUNNAR

    It's the perfect way to start the day, surrounded by the lush greenery and fresh mountain air of Munnar. The national park offers stunning views and a chance to spot some of the local wildlife. Plus, getting some exercise in before lunch will make it that much more enjoyable. Don't forget to pack some water and snacks for the hike, and be sure to take plenty of photos to remember this beautiful experience.

  • DAY 4 - MUNNAR TO THEKKADY

    After Breakfast drive to Thekkady, explore the lush greenery of Thekkady by taking a nature walk or going on a jungle safari. Keep an eye out for various wildlife species such as tigers, leopards, and bison. Don't forget to visit the spice plantations in the area and learn about the different spices grown there. End your day with a relaxing evening watching a traditional Kathakali dance performance or enjoying a delicious meal at a local restaurant.

  • DAY 5 - ALLEPPEY

    After breakfast, we will drive to Alleppey, a town renowned for its boat races and backwaters. Along the way, we will visit rubber and coffee plantations. We will spend the night on a local houseboat that will sail through the backwaters of Wayanad Lake. This unique experience will allow us to immerse ourselves in the beauty of the surroundings as we cruise through the night.

  • DAY 6 - KOVALAM BEACH

    After enjoying breakfast, we will check out from the houseboat and drive to Kovalam Beach. In the evening, we will have the opportunity to relax and unwind at the beautiful beach.

  • DAY 7 - RELAX BEACH

    The Relax Beach Kovalam full day experience offers a tranquil and rejuvenating escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. Located in the picturesque town of Kovalam, known for its pristine beaches and serene atmosphere, this full-day package provides guests with a complete relaxation retreat. The day begins with a welcome drink upon arrival at the beachside resort, followed by a leisurely morning at the sandy shores where guests can enjoy sunbathing, swimming, or simply lounging under a shady umbrella. A sumptuous buffet lunch is served at the on-site restaurant offering fresh seafood and local delicacies. After lunch, guests have the option to partake in various activities such as yoga sessions, spa treatments, or exploring the nearby attractions. As evening approaches, a calming sunset cruise awaits to end the day on a peaceful note. Overall, the Relax Beach Kovalam full-day experience offers an ideal blend of tranquility and luxury for those seeking a well-deserved break from their daily routine.

  • DAY 8 - BACK TO NEXT DESTINATION

    You have the option to extend your trip to either Chennai and Pondicherry or Mumbai and Goa.

**FAQ - KERALA TOUR **

1. What are the best times to visit Kerala?

The ideal times to visit Kerala are from October to February, when the weather is pleasant and suitable for sightseeing. The monsoon season, from June to August, offers lush greenery but may not be suitable for all travelers.

2. What are the must-see attractions in Kerala?

Key attractions include the backwaters of Alleppey, the hill stations of Munnar, the historic city of Kochi, the wildlife sanctuaries in Periyar, and the beautiful beaches of Varkala and Kovalam.

3. What types of tours are available in Kerala?

Kerala offers a variety of tours, including cultural tours, adventure tours, backwater cruises, wildlife safaris, and wellness retreats focusing on Ayurveda.

4. How can I travel within Kerala?

Transportation options include local buses, auto-rickshaws, taxis, and rental cars. Additionally, houseboat cruises are a popular way to explore the backwaters.

5. Is Kerala suitable for family vacations?

Yes, Kerala is a family-friendly destination with activities suitable for all ages, including nature walks, boat rides, and cultural experiences that can be enjoyed by the whole family.

6. What should I pack for my trip to Kerala?

Pack lightweight, breathable clothing suitable for warm weather. Dont forget essentials like sunscreen, insect repellent, comfortable walking shoes, and a raincoat if traveling during the monsoon season.

7. Are there any specific cultural practices I should be aware of?

Kerala has a rich cultural heritage. When visiting temples, dress modestly and follow local customs. Its also respectful to ask before taking photographs of people or sacred sites.

8. What local cuisine should I try while in Kerala?

Dont miss out on traditional dishes like Kerala Sadya (a feast served on a banana leaf), seafood curry, appam with stew, and various spicy vegetarian dishes. Fresh coconut and local spices are staples in Kerala cuisine.

9. Is it safe to travel in Kerala?

Kerala is generally considered safe for tourists. However, as with any travel, its advisable to take standard precautions, such as safeguarding your belongings and being aware of your surroundings.

10. How do I book a tour in Kerala?

You can book tours through travel agencies, online platforms, or directly with local tour operators. Its recommended to read reviews and compare packages to ensure a quality experience.

Frequently Ask Questions: India Tours

What are the best times to visit India?

The best times to visit India generally fall between October to March, when the weather is cooler and more pleasant for traveling. However, the ideal time may vary based on the region you plan to visit.

What modes of transportation are available for traveling within India?

India offers various modes of transportation, including domestic flights, trains, buses, and taxis. The Indian Railways is a popular choice for long distances, while local buses and auto-rickshaws are commonly used for short trips within cities.

Is it safe to travel in India?

Traveling in India is generally safe for tourists. However, it is advisable to stay vigilant, follow local guidelines, and take standard safety precautions, such as avoiding isolated areas at night and securing your belongings.

How can I book train tickets in India?

Train tickets can be booked online through the Indian Railways website or through various travel booking platforms. It is recommended to book tickets in advance, especially during peak travel seasons.

What is the best way to experience Indian cuisine while traveling?

To fully experience Indian cuisine, consider dining at local restaurants, trying street food, and participating in cooking classes. Each region has its unique dishes, so be adventurous and explore the local flavors.

Should I hire a guide for my tours in India?

Hiring a local guide can enhance your travel experience by providing insights into local culture, history, and attractions. It is especially beneficial in cities with rich heritage and complex histories.

What cultural etiquettes should I be aware of while traveling in India?

It is important to respect local customs and traditions. Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites, and be mindful of local practices, such as removing shoes before entering homes or temples.

Are there any specific health precautions I should take before visiting India?

Before traveling to India, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional regarding vaccinations and health precautions. Carry a basic first aid kit and be cautious with drinking water and street food.

Can I use credit cards in India?

Credit cards are widely accepted in urban areas and major tourist destinations, but it is advisable to carry cash for small purchases and in rural areas. ATMs are available in most cities.

What should I pack for my trip to India?

Pack lightweight, breathable clothing suitable for the climate, comfortable shoes for walking, and a travel adapter for electronics. Also, consider bringing a reusable water bottle and a basic first aid kit.

Are there any restrictions on photography in India?

While photography is generally allowed, certain places, particularly religious sites and military installations, may have restrictions. Always ask for permission when photographing people and be respectful of local customs.

What are some must-visit destinations in India?

India is home to numerous must-visit destinations, including the Taj Mahal in Agra, Jaipur's palaces, Kerala's backwaters, the beaches of Goa, and the spiritual city of Varanasi and Dharamshala. Each region offers unique experiences and attractions.
